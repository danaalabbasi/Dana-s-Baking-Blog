Babka Bread

WHAT IS IT?

Babka is an Eastern European specialty, it is a yeasted bread that is usually filled with chocolate, cinnamon, or fruit. This chocolate babka is prepared with enriched bread dough, like challah or brioche, filled with decadent chocolate. Every mouthful is buttery, soft, and full of chocolate goodness. It is my all-time favorite bread for the holidays, no one will be able to resist a warm bite of this chocolate babka. Here’s everything you need to know about how to make the ideal chocolate babka.

Ingredients

FOR THE DOUGH
½ cup of softened unsalted butter
½ cup of warm water
¼ cup of sugar
1 teaspoon of sea salt
1 teaspoon of instant dry or rapid-rising yeast
3 large eggs
2 ¾ cups of all-purpose flour
FOR THE CHOCOLATE FILLING
1 ¼ cups of bittersweet chocolate
½ cup of unsalted butter
1⁄3 cup of heavy cream
½ cup of powdered sugar
1⁄3 cup of unsweetened cocoa powder
A pinch of salt
FOR THE SYRUP
¼ cup of water
6 tablespoons of sugar
A pinch of salt

Instructions

STEP 1: MAKING THE DOUGH

In a large bowl, whisk together warm water, sugar, salt, and yeast. Set it aside for 5 minutes to allow the yeast to activate. Add the softened butter, eggs, and flour, then stir until the flour is absorbed and everything is fully combined. Remove the dough from the bowl and knead by hand for approximately 8 minutes or until the dough is smooth and no longer lumpy. Form the dough into a tight ball place it in a greased or lightly dusted bowl, cover it with a kitchen towel, and allow it to rest in a warm spot for about an hour.

STEP 2: MAKING THE FILLING

In a medium saucepan melt the chocolate, butter, and sugar over medium heat then whisk in the heavy cream, cocoa powder, and salt on low heat until completely smooth. Transfer to a heatproof bowl and set aside to cool and thicken up.

STEP 3: SHAPING AND PROOFING

Transfer the dough to a clean work surface, lightly dusted with flour. Knead and roll out the dough into a rectangle, it should be about 1⁄8 – inch thick. Using a spatula, spread the filling over the dough leaving ½ inch border. Using both your hands start by slowly rolling it up from the bottom along the longer edge and slightly pulling back to make the roulade as tight as possible. Turn the roulade vertically, so one end of it is facing you, using a sharp knife cut the roulade in half lengthwise. Turn the cut side up and twist/braid the strands together, while keeping the cut side facing up. Transfer the babka to a greased and dusted loaf pan, cover it with a kitchen towel and allow it to proof for 1 – 1 ½ hours in a warm spot.

STEP 4: BAKE THE BABKAS

Preheat the oven to 350°F/175°C and bake for 30 – 45 minutes, until the dough is golden brown, and the chocolate is dark on top.

STEP 5: MAKING THE SYRUP

In a small saucepan combine the sugar, water, and salt over high heat and bring to a boil. As soon as the sugar dissolves, remove it from the heat and set it aside to cool.

STEP 6: DRIZZLE THE SYRUP

As soon as the babka comes out of the oven brush or drizzle the syrup over it and allow it to cool in the pan before slicing and serving.
